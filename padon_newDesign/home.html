Food Delicacies

Cagayan de Oro has a diverse and delicious food scene, and there are many local dishes that visitors must try. For example, the city's version of the lechon, a roasted pig, is known for its crispy skin and juicy meat. Suman, a traditional Filipino rice cake, is often served with tsokolate, a hot chocolate drink made from tablea (cocoa tablets).

Pastel, a sweet bun filled with yema (sweet custard) or meat, is a popular pastry in the city. Cagayan de Oro is also known for its seafood dishes, such as kinilaw (raw fish marinated in vinegar and spices) and grilled squid. Finally, chicharon, a crispy fried pork rind, is another popular snack in the city. (See Food Delicacies Lists)

Tourist Attraction

Cagayan de Oro has many popular tourist attractions, including White Water Rafting, which is known as the "Whitewater Rafting Capital of the Philippines". The city's Museo de Oro showcases the rich culture and history of the Higaunon tribe and the city's early settlers. St. Augustine Cathedral, built in the 19th century, is a symbol of the city's strong Catholic heritage.

The Gardens of Malasag Eco-Tourism Village is a nature park that offers a scenic view of the city and its surrounding mountains. Finally, Mapawa Nature Park is an eco-tourism destination that offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, ziplining, and horseback riding. (See Tourist Destination Lists)
